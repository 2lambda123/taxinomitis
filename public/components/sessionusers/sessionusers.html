Questions

Why am I trying this?

I know that getting younger students to remember (and type in!) yet another username and password can be a challenge.

Setting up and managing a class account is extra work that busy teachers don't always have time for.

I'm trying this as it would mean a class could easily be run with nothing to setup ahead of time, and no passwords for kids to type in.

Am I going to replace the teacher/student logins with this?

No. I still think there are many benefits to the existing student accounts.

Students can save their work and come back to it in a later lesson. They can work on a project together with other students in their class. They can log on from home and show their family what they've made.

I think this “Try it now” idea is a nice alternative option, not a replacement.

Is there a limit to how many people can use this at once?

Yes.

For now, at least.

I'm going to start small - test it with a very small limit with a few users. I'll increase it gradually as I see how it works out.

Why are image projects excluded from this?

Cost. Sorry.

I'm not sure what the usage of this will be like, so I'm going to start small and cheap and see how it goes. And the Watson Assistant APIs I'm using to train text projects are pretty cheap, so I'm thinking it's a safe place to start.

What does the "Log out" button do?

It deletes your temporary account and all of your work.

What if you don't click "Log out"?

Your temporary login will work for about four hours. After that, it will be deleted along with all of your work.

Is four hours the right amount of time for a temporary, one-use logon? Let me know what you think.
